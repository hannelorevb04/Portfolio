Arduino Piano

For our Arduino project, we built a piano allowing users to play music using push buttons. The piano is equipped with a buzzer for sound output and an LCD screen to display the keys being pressed. Additionally, there's a feature enabling users to automatically play and learn songs.

How did we approach it?

  • Exploration: In the exploration phase, we researched the technical capabilities of the Arduino platform to build a functional piano. We identified the necessary components such as push buttons, a buzzer, and an LCD screen. By studying existing DIY projects and documentation, we gained insight into the best approach to realize our idea.
  • Execution: The execution phase involved implementing the various functions of the piano. We started by connecting the push buttons to the Arduino to simulate the piano keys. Then, we linked the buzzer to produce the sound of the piano when the keys are pressed.
  • Functional description:
    • Input and output: We connected push buttons acting as piano keys. When a button is pressed, a signal is sent to the Arduino, which then activates the buzzer to produce the corresponding sound.
    • Serial communication: Communication between the push buttons and the buzzer is essential for the operation of the piano. With each press of a button, the Arduino sends a signal to the buzzer to generate the correct sound.
    • LCD: The LCD screen is integrated to display the notes. When a key is pressed, the screen shows which note is played. Additionally, users can press a button to see an automatically played song displayed on the screen and attempt to play it.
